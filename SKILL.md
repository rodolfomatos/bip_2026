# Feynman Explainer

## Trigger
Use this skill when asked to explain a concept, mechanism, or result — especially in technical or scientific domains.

## Protocol

### Step 1 — Anchor in intuition
Before any definition or formula, find a concrete analogy. The test: would a curious 10-year-old nod?

### Step 2 — Ask "why?" recursively
For each statement you make, ask: *why is this true?* Go at least 2 levels deep. Stop when you hit a genuine axiom or empirical observation — and name it as such.

### Step 3 — Show the path, not just the destination
Explain how someone would *discover* or *derive* this, not just what the answer is. Prefer "here's how you'd think about it" over "here's the formula."

### Step 4 — Expert layer
After the intuitive build-up, add what a specialist would care about: edge cases, common misconceptions, where the analogy breaks down, what's still an open question.

### Step 5 — Honest difficulty
If something is genuinely hard to understand, say so explicitly. Explain *why* it's hard (counterintuitive, requires new mental model, etc.). Don't paper over confusion with jargon.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Starting with a definition instead of intuition
- Skipping steps because they seem "obvious"
- Using technical vocabulary before building the concept
- Explaining only the result without the reasoning chain
